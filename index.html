Recommended ETFs

VOO

VOO, the Vanguard S&P 500 ETF, is a popular, low-cost exchange-traded fund designed to mirror the performance of the S&P 500 Index, providing broad exposure to 500 of the largest U.S. companies for long-term growth. It's managed passively, holding stocks in similar proportions to the index, and offers significant diversification with minimal fees, making it a core holding for many investors seeking overall U.S. stock market returns.

QQQM / FTEC / VGT

QQQM, the Invesco NASDAQ 100 ETF, is a low-cost fund tracking the NASDAQ-100 Index, offering exposure to 100 of the largest non-financial companies on the Nasdaq exchange, primarily in tech and growth sectors like Apple, Microsoft, and NVIDIA. FTEC (Fidelity MSCI Information Technology Index ETF) and VGT (Vanguard Information Technology ETF) provide broad exposure to the U.S. tech sector with low expenses, ideal for long-term growth. Combined, these ETFs form a tech-focused backbone of a portfolio.

SCHD

SCHD (Schwab U.S. Dividend Equity ETF) is a low-cost ETF that tracks the Dow Jones U.S. Dividend 100 Index, focusing on high-quality, large-cap U.S. companies with strong, consistent dividends, serving as a core holding for diversified portfolios.

VXUS

VXUS (Vanguard Total International Stock ETF) is a low-cost fund offering broad exposure to global stocks outside the U.S., covering over 98% of the world's investable non-U.S. market capitalization in developed and emerging markets. Ideal for long-term diversification.

Individual Stocks

SLV

SLV (iShares Silver Trust) provides exposure to silver, a finite resource that can fight against inflation and balance your portfolio when markets are volatile. Use for diversification.

GLD

GLD (SPDR Gold Shares) provides exposure to gold, a classic hedge against inflation and market uncertainty. Gold is finite and helps diversify portfolios alongside traditional equities.

Tip: Set up a foundation of ETFs before buying individual stocks. Individual stocks are riskier; do thorough research before investing.
