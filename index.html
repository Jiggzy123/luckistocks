Fundamentals

  • Roth IRA vs Individual Brokerage Account: A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, but has contribution limits and rules. A normal individual brokerage account has no contribution limits or withdrawal restrictions, but you pay taxes on dividends and capital gains.
  • Roth IRA vs 401(k): A Roth IRA is an individual retirement account you open yourself with after-tax money and tax-free growth. A 401(k) is usually employer-sponsored, often includes a company match, and can be either pre-tax or Roth depending on the plan.
  • What is an ETF: An ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) is a collection of stocks or assets bundled together that trades like a single stock, allowing instant diversification with low fees.
  • What are dividends: Dividends are payments companies make to shareholders, usually from profits, and can be taken as cash or reinvested to compound returns over time.
  • Why should you invest: Investing allows your money to grow faster than inflation through compound interest, helping build long-term wealth instead of losing purchasing power by holding cash.
