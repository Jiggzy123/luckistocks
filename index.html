Fundamentals

  • Roth IRA vs Individual Brokerage Account: A Roth IRA allows your money to grow and be withdrawn tax-free in retirement if rules are followed. An individual brokerage account has no limits or restrictions, but you must pay taxes on dividends and gains.
  • Roth IRA vs 401(k): A Roth IRA is opened by you personally, while a 401(k) is employer-sponsored and often includes matching contributions. 401(k)s may have limited investment choices, while Roth IRAs usually offer more flexibility.
  • What is an ETF? An ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) is a basket of many stocks bundled into one investment, allowing diversification with a single purchase.
  • What are Dividends? Dividends are payments made to investors from company profits. They can be reinvested to increase compound growth or taken as income.
  • Why Should You Invest? Investing helps your money grow over time, beat inflation, and take advantage of compound interest — which rewards consistency and time in the market.
