Fundamentals

  • Roth IRA vs Individual Account: A Roth IRA is a tax-advantaged retirement account where contributions are made with after-tax money, but qualified withdrawals are completely tax-free. An individual (taxable) brokerage account has no tax advantages — you pay taxes on dividends and capital gains — but offers full flexibility with no age or income restrictions.
  • Roth IRA vs 401(k): A Roth IRA is opened individually and funded by you, while a 401(k) is employer-sponsored and often includes a company match. Roth IRAs grow tax-free with flexible investment choices, while 401(k)s usually have limited fund options but higher contribution limits.
  • What is an ETF? An ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) is a collection of stocks bundled into one investment that trades like a stock. ETFs provide instant diversification, lower risk than single stocks, and typically have low expense ratios.
  • What are Dividends? Dividends are payments companies make to shareholders from their profits. They can be paid in cash or reinvested automatically and are commonly used to generate passive income or accelerate long-term growth.
  • Why Should You Invest? Investing allows your money to grow over time through compound interest, helping you beat inflation and build long-term wealth. Starting early gives your money more time to work for you, even with smaller contributions.
