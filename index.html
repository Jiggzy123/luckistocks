Fundamentals

  • Roth IRA vs Individual Account: Roth IRAs grow tax-free but have contribution and income limits. Individual brokerage accounts have no limits, but dividends and capital gains are taxable.
  • Roth IRA vs 401k: Roth IRAs are after-tax and tax-free later. 401ks are usually pre-tax and often include employer matching.
  • What is an ETF: ETFs are baskets of stocks bundled together. They provide instant diversification and usually have low fees.
  • What are dividends: Dividends are payments companies make to shareholders from profits.
  • Why invest: Investing lets your money compound over time and beat inflation.

Stocks Explained

A stock represents ownership in a single company. When you buy a stock, you are buying a small piece of that business.

Stocks can be more volatile than ETFs because your investment depends on one company’s performance. However, they also offer higher potential returns if the company grows significantly.

Stocks can generate money through price growth and dividends, but they require more research and risk tolerance. Long-term investors often mix stocks with ETFs for balance.

Recommended ETFs

VOO

Tracks the S&P 500, giving exposure to 500 of the largest U.S. companies. Considered a core long-term holding with strong historical returns.

VXUS

Provides international exposure outside the U.S., helping diversify against U.S.-only risk.

SCHD

Focused on dividend-paying companies. Often used for income and stability.

Technology ETFs

QQQM: Tracks the Nasdaq-100, emphasizing high-growth tech and innovation companies.

FTEC: Broad U.S. technology exposure with a lower expense ratio.

VGT: Heavy concentration in large-cap technology companies.

Note: Usually best to choose one tech ETF to avoid overlap.
