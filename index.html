Fundamentals

  • Roth IRA vs Individual Account: Roth IRAs grow tax-free but have income and contribution limits. Individual brokerage accounts have no limits but dividends and capital gains are taxable.
  • Roth IRA vs 401k: Roth IRAs use after-tax money and grow tax-free. 401ks are usually pre-tax and often include employer matching.
  • What is an ETF: An ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) is a basket of investments that trades like a stock, offering instant diversification.
  • What are dividends: Dividends are profit payments companies distribute to shareholders, either as cash or reinvested.
  • Why invest: Investing helps grow wealth over time, beat inflation, and take advantage of compound interest.

What Are Stocks?

A stock represents ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you own a small piece of that business. If the company grows and becomes more profitable, the value of your stock can increase. Some stocks also pay dividends, providing passive income.
