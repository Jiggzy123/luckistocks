Fundamentals

  • Roth IRA vs Individual Account: A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, but has contribution limits and income restrictions. An individual brokerage account has no contribution limits or income requirements, but you pay taxes on dividends and capital gains.
  • Roth IRA vs 401k: A Roth IRA is funded with after-tax money and grows tax-free, while a 401k is typically pre-tax and taxed upon withdrawal. 401ks often include employer matching.
  • What is an ETF: An ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) is a collection of stocks or assets that trades like a stock and provides instant diversification.
  • What are dividends: Dividends are payments companies make to shareholders from profits, either as income or reinvested.
  • Why invest: Investing allows your money to grow over time through compound interest and helps beat inflation.
