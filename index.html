Individual Brokerage Account vs Roth IRA

Individual Brokerage Account

An individual brokerage account offers maximum flexibility. You can invest as much as you want and withdraw your money at any time without penalties. However, you pay taxes on dividends and on gains when you sell investments.

Roth IRA

A Roth IRA is designed for long-term retirement investing. Contributions are made with after-tax money, but all qualified growth and withdrawals are tax-free.

Taxes & Compound Interest

Removing taxes allows compound interest to work more efficiently over decades. Even small tax differences can result in significantly higher long-term growth.
