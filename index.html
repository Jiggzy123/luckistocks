Fundamentals

  • Roth IRA vs Individual Account: Roth IRAs grow tax-free but have contribution and income limits. Individual brokerage accounts have no limits, but dividends and capital gains are taxable.
  • Roth IRA vs 401k: Roth IRAs are funded with after-tax dollars and grow tax-free. 401ks are usually pre-tax and often include employer matching.
  • What is an ETF: An ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) is a basket of assets that trades like a stock, offering instant diversification.
  • What are dividends: Dividends are payments companies make to shareholders from profits.
  • Why invest: Investing allows your money to grow over time, beat inflation, and benefit from compounding.

Fundamentals: Stocks Explained

A stock represents ownership in a company. When you buy a share of stock, you own a small portion of that business. As the company grows, becomes more profitable, or increases revenue, the value of your shares may increase.

Stocks can generate returns in two main ways: price appreciation (the stock goes up in value) and dividends (payments companies distribute to shareholders). Stocks tend to be more volatile than ETFs but offer higher growth potential.

Long-term investors typically benefit from holding high-quality stocks or broad market ETFs rather than attempting to trade frequently.
