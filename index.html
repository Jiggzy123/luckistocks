Fundamentals

  • Roth IRA vs Individual Account: A Roth IRA allows tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, but has income and contribution limits. Individual brokerage accounts have no limits but are taxable.
  • Roth IRA vs 401k: Roth IRAs are after-tax and tax-free later. 401ks are pre-tax and often include employer matching.
  • What is an ETF: ETFs bundle many stocks into one fund for diversification and lower risk.
  • What are dividends: Payments companies distribute to shareholders, often quarterly.
  • Why invest: Investing builds wealth over time and combats inflation.

Stocks Explained

A stock represents ownership in a company. Stock prices grow as companies grow profits and expand.

Stocks can offer high returns but are more volatile than ETFs. Long-term holding reduces risk.

Recommended ETFs

Core Market ETFs

  • VOO: Tracks the S&P 500 and represents the backbone of many portfolios.
  • VXUS: Adds international exposure to reduce U.S.-only risk.
  • SCHD: Focuses on reliable dividend-paying companies.

Technology ETFs

  • QQQM: Nasdaq-100 growth-focused ETF.
  • FTEC: Broad tech exposure with a lower expense ratio.
  • VGT: Highly concentrated long-term tech growth ETF.
